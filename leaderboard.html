What the data shows

Six signals from
59 assessments

01
Moats are strong — pricing is not
Average SMS (structural moat) of 3.87 versus BMS (business model) of only 1.92. Companies have built real product depth but are leaving revenue expansion on the table with seat-based pricing.
02
Almost no market creators
Only 4 of 59 companies are defining a genuinely new market. 61% are displacing incumbents — competing for existing budget rather than expanding the pie, which compresses win rates and pricing power.
03
The cohort is pre-PMF confirmation
88% are Early stage — pre-$5M ARR. SPS functions as a leading indicator here: structural position now predicts who will break out once go-to-market intensity increases.
04
Scores cluster tightly
The full cohort spans just 1.37 SPS points (2.28–3.65). No Elite tier company yet. The Indian B2B AI market is in a formative phase — structural separation hasn't happened.
05
Workflow lock-in dominates moats
The most common moat combination — data network effects + workflow lock-in — appears in 33% of companies. It's effective but also the most common: not yet a differentiator at the cohort level.
06
BMS is the highest-leverage lever
Shifting from seat-based to outcome or consumption-based pricing is a direct +0.4 SPS uplift. It's the single most actionable change a portfolio company can make to improve structural position score.
