The problem

Don't understand growth quality

How it works

Three steps to controlled growth

A systematic approach to predictable outcomes. GrowFitt validates and aligns your growth ambition with your system's actual fitness.

01

Measure your growth fitness

Your Growth Fitness Score (GFS) is derived from three observable signals — growth quality, retention quality, and efficiency quality. Built from internal data, operator input, and market signals. Normalised to a 0–5 scale, adjusted for stage and strategy.

02

Define your optimal growth rate

Your GFS determines a controlled operating range — the growth rate your system can actually sustain. Not an arbitrary target, but a mathematically derived range. Grow within it and you scale efficiently. Push beyond it and the system degrades.

03

Track and control — continuously

Fit is not a fixed point, it's a moving target. As you improve your system, your range expands. GrowFitt tracks your GFS over time so you can improve the system, expand the range, and align growth — quarter by quarter.

The model

Built on evidence.
Personalised to your system.

The Growth Fitness Score is not a survey or a consultant's opinion. It is a quantitative model trained and pressure-tested against the growth data of publicly listed companies worldwide — across the US, India, Europe, and beyond.

The model analyses how high-performing companies actually scale — what separates those that sustain growth over 8–12 quarters from those that break — and translates those patterns into a single, comparable score for your business.

Your GFS is then personalised to your stage, market, and GTM motion. A Series A company is not scored against a late-stage SaaS business. The model adapts to your context before it renders a verdict.

Quantitative inputs
What the numbers say
Revenue growth, net and gross retention, gross margin, free cash flow, sales efficiency, CAC payback — the operating signals that don't lie.
Qualitative inputs
What the numbers miss
Inbound pull strength, deal quality trajectory, cohort health trends — the leading indicators that quantitative data alone can't capture.
